3. Ranking System — Two Layers

Layer 1: TrueSkill (The Real Signal)

Why TrueSkill? It was designed by Microsoft Research specifically for rating individuals in team games. Unlike Elo (built for 1v1), TrueSkill uses Bayesian inference to figure out how much each individual contributed to a team's win or loss. Each player has μ (estimated skill) and σ (uncertainty). The display rating is μ - 3σ — a conservative lower bound that rewards consistent play. We use openskill (patent-free equivalent).

Layer 2: Weekly Power Index — WPI (The Fun Layer)

Computed every Monday. Combines three factors into one score:

50%
🎯 Skill Score
Your current TrueSkill ordinal (μ - 3σ) normalized to 0–100 across all players. This is the stable backbone — it doesn't swing wildly week to week.
25%
🔥 Form Score
How you performed this week vs. what your rating predicted. If you won matches you were "expected" to lose, your form is high. Rewards players who punch above their weight.
25%
💪 Impact Score
Do your partners win more when they play WITH you vs. WITHOUT you? High impact = you elevate everyone around you. Uses all-time data, requires 10+ matches per pair.
WPI = 0.50 × Skill + 0.25 × Form + 0.25 × Impact → Ranked #1 to #6
Minimum threshold: A player must play at least 5 matches in a week to receive a WPI ranking. Below that, only raw TrueSkill is shown. Impact Score defaults to 50 (neutral) for pairs with fewer than 10 total matches together.

5. Tech Stack

FastAPI
Async Python backend with auto-generated Swagger docs, Pydantic validation
SQLAlchemy 2.0
ORM with Alembic migrations. SQLite today, PostgreSQL migration trivial.
SQLite (WAL)
Zero cost, single-file database. Sufficient for 6 users and ~15 matches/day.
openskill
Patent-free TrueSkill implementation. PlackettLuce model for team-based ranking.
Jinja2 + HTMX
Server-rendered templates with interactive partial updates. No React build step needed.
Chart.js
Lightweight charting via CDN. Trend lines, sparklines, rating history charts.
Railway
Free tier hosting with persistent disk for SQLite. Git-based auto-deploy.
GitHub Actions
CI (pytest + ruff on push) and weekly cron job for WPI computation.
Docker
Dockerfile + docker-compose for consistent local development setup.
